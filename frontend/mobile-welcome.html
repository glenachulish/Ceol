Welcome to Ceòl

A traditional music library you actually want to use.

You've just signed up to Ceòl. This is your personal library of traditional Irish and Scottish music — tunes, sheet music, audio, recordings, sets, the lot. Nobody else can see what's in it.

The app runs on a Raspberry Pi sitting in Callum's house, so you're not on a cloud service that might disappear next year. Everything you add belongs to you and stays there.

How to get around

The bottom of the screen has tabs: Library for your tunes, Sets for groupings of tunes that go together, Collections for broader categories ("Diggers tunes", "Cape Breton stuff", whatever you want), and Import for adding new tunes.

Your library starts empty. Let's add something to it.

Add your first tune

The easiest way to get tunes into Ceòl is to copy them from TheSession.org — a free online catalogue of traditional tunes. Here's the six-step recipe:

  1. Open TheSession.org in another browser tab and find a tune you like.
  2. On the tune page, look for the X:1 block near the top — that's the ABC notation. Tap and hold to select it all, then copy.
  3. Back in Ceòl, tap Import at the bottom.
  4. Tap the Paste ABC tab.
  5. Paste what you copied into the big text box.
  6. Tap Import.

Your new tune should appear in your library with sheet music and all the metadata filled in.

Hear it played

Tap any tune in your library to open it. You'll see the sheet music rendered properly with bar lines, and a play button at the top. Tap play — Ceòl will play the tune through your phone's speakers using a flute sound. Not a perfect performance, but very useful for sanity-checking that you've got the right tune.

If you can't hear anything on an iPhone, check the silent switch on the side of the phone is off (the orange line shouldn't be showing). iOS treats web audio as quiet by default.

Mark your favourites

On every tune card in your library there are two little buttons in the corner:

  • 👍 Favourite — tunes you love. Filter by these later with the Favourites filter.
  • 📌 Hitlist — tunes you're working on. Use this as your practice list for the week.

You can also star-rate tunes 1–5 to remember which settings you prefer.
