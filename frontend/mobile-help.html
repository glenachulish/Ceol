Finding tunes in your library

The Library tab is your main view. Tunes appear as cards with the title, type (reel, jig, etc.), key, and a hint of the sheet music.

Searching

Tap the search bar at the top and start typing. Search looks at the tune title, alternative names, and composer. It searches as you type — no need to hit a button.

Filters

The filter row sits under the search bar. You can filter by:

  • Type — reel, jig, hornpipe, slow air, etc.
  • Key — G major, D minor, etc.
  • Mode — major, dorian, mixolydian.
  • Rating — minimum stars (1–5).
  • 📌 Hitlist — only tunes on your current hitlist.
  • 👍 Favourites — only ones you've marked as favourite.
  • Collection — only tunes in a specific collection.

Filters combine — you can have "Reels in D, rated 4 or above, on the hitlist" all at once. Tap a filter again to clear it.

Reading a tune

Tap any tune card to open the full view. You'll see:

  • Sheet music — rendered from the ABC notation. Pinch to zoom.
  • Play / pause / stop controls at the top.
  • Tempo slider — slow it down for learning.
  • Transpose — shift the tune up or down a number of semitones. The sheet music updates live.
  • Metadata — type, key, composer, where it came from, any notes you've added.

The playback uses a flute sound by default. It's a synthesiser — useful for hearing the shape of a tune, not for replacing a real recording.

Practising — the loop, tempo, hitlist

The bar-range loop

The killer practice feature. On the sheet music, tap a bar to start the range, then tap another bar to end it. The playback now loops just those bars over and over until you tell it to stop.

Combine that with the tempo slider and you can hammer a tricky phrase at half speed until it sticks, then speed it back up.

Highlights

You can highlight specific bars on a tune to mark them as difficult. Tap a bar and choose Highlight. Useful for remembering "this is the bit I always trip on".

The hitlist

The 📌 hitlist is your current practice list. Mark tunes you're actively learning. Filter the library by 📌 to see just your hitlist, then work through it.

When a tune lands properly under your fingers, take it off the hitlist (tap the 📌 again on the card) — that's the small win.

Adding tunes from TheSession.org

TheSession.org is the biggest free catalogue of traditional Irish tunes. There are a few ways to import from it but the most reliable is copy-paste:

  1. Open TheSession.org in a browser, find the tune.
  2. On the tune page, scroll to a setting you like. Each setting shows ABC notation in a code-style block starting with X:1.
  3. Select the whole ABC block — from X:1 down to the last bar of the tune — and copy.
  4. In Ceòl, tap Import at the bottom.
  5. Tap the Paste ABC tab.
  6. Paste into the text box. Tap Import.

The tune lands in your library with title, type, key, mode, and composer all filled in from the ABC headers.

There's also a direct TheSession.org import tab that searches by tune name, but the copy-paste route gives you more control over which setting you grab. Most people end up using paste-ABC for that reason.

Other ways to add tunes

Tap Import at the bottom and you'll see a row of tabs. The most useful besides Paste ABC:

  • ABC File — upload one or more .abc files from your phone.
  • Folder — upload a whole folder of ABC files at once. Good for migrating from another app.
  • Photos — take a photo of sheet music, Ceòl will attempt to read it.
  • PDF Files / PDF Book — pull tunes out of PDF tunebooks via OCR.
  • Ceol file — import a .ceol.json export from another Ceòl user.
  • TheCraic — import from the TheCraic iOS app.

The other tabs (Music Scanner, YouTube, FlutefFling, Dropbox) all work too — explore them as you need them.

When you import in bulk, Ceòl shows a Recently Imported set at the top of your Sets view, grouping the batch together so you can find what you just added.

Adding audio to a tune

Attaching a real recording — your own playing, a session you were at, a YouTube clip — is one of the most useful things you can do for a tune. It turns a piece of sheet music into something living.

Upload an audio file from your phone

  1. Open the tune.
  2. Scroll down to the attachments section.
  3. Tap 🎧 Add audio.
  4. Tap 📂 Choose audio file… and pick the file from your phone's storage.
  5. Wait for the upload. MP3, M4A, WAV, OGG, AAC, FLAC all work.

Once attached, the file appears with a play button and a label ("audio 1", "audio 2", etc — you can rename them). Tap to play in-line.

WAV files get automatically converted to MP3 on upload to save space. The original is replaced. AIFF files unfortunately don't work yet — convert them to MP3 first.

Link a YouTube video

Same Add audio menu, switch to the YouTube tab, paste the URL.

Adding sheet music photos

If you have a paper score, a setting written out by hand, or a printed page you want to keep with the tune, you can attach it as an image:

  1. Open the tune.
  2. Find the sheet music attachment button.
  3. Either choose a photo from your library or take one with the camera.

The photo lives alongside the auto-generated sheet music. Useful when the printed version has fingering, ornament marks, or notes written on it.

Sets

A set is a sequence of tunes you play one after the other — the bread and butter of session playing. Ceòl has two ways to build them.

Build a set from scratch (freeform)

  1. Tap Sets at the bottom.
  2. Tap + New Set.
  3. Give it a name (and optional notes) and tap Create.
  4. Open the new set. Search your library and add tunes one by one in the order you want to play them.
  5. Drag to reorder if needed.

This is what you want if you already know which tunes go together — "Drowsy Maggie into The Star of Munster into The Banshee."

Build a set with Circle of Fifths help

If you're not sure what'd flow well after a particular tune, Ceòl can suggest:

  1. In the Sets view, tap 🎵 Build a Set.
  2. Pick a starting tune.
  3. Ceòl shows you tunes in compatible keys — the same key, the relative minor/major, neighbours on the circle of fifths.
  4. Add the ones you like, in order.
  5. Name and save.

Useful when you've got a big library and want to find unexpected combinations.

Playing a set

Open a set and tap play — Ceòl plays through every tune in sequence. Each tune is played the usual number of times (twice through is the default for reels and jigs), then moves on.

Collections

Collections are looser groupings than sets. A set is "these three tunes, played in this order, twice each." A collection is "all my Diggers tunes" or "tunes I learned in 2024" or "Cape Breton fiddle stuff" — a label you stick on a bunch of tunes for finding them later.

Creating a collection

  1. Tap Collections at the bottom.
  2. Tap + New Collection.
  3. Name it. Tap Create.

Adding tunes to a collection

Open the collection and use the Add Tunes button to search your library and pick. A tune can be in as many collections as you like — they're just labels, not folders.

Filtering by collection

Back in the Library, the Collection filter lets you see only tunes in a specific collection.

Versions — keeping different settings

Lots of traditional tunes have multiple settings: TheSession.org might list five different versions of The Abbey Reel. Ceòl lets you keep more than one as related rows so the library doesn't fill up with what look like duplicates.

What versions do

One tune is the parent; the others are versions of it. They appear stacked on a single card in the library with a count ("3 versions"). Open the card and switch between them.

How to make a version

  • When importing a tune that's clearly another setting of one you already have, Ceòl will offer to attach it as a version rather than a new tune.
  • You can also manually merge two tunes via the versions panel on a tune detail page.
  • Each version can have a version label ("my setting", "Liz Carroll's version", "Diggers version") so you remember which is which.

Achievements

The Achievements tab shows a timeline of milestones — first tune added, first set built, tune count crossing 50/100/500, that sort of thing. Pure dopamine. Don't take it too seriously, but it's nice to look back on.

Your account & password

Forgot your password?

From the login page, tap Forgot password? under the password field. Enter your username or email. An admin gets notified, and they'll send you a reset link out-of-band (iMessage, WhatsApp, whatever). Tap the link, set a new password, you're in.

Logging out

There's a Logout option in the main menu. Worth doing if you're on a shared device.

Admin (if you have it)

If your account is marked as admin, you can visit /admin on the site. This page lets you:

  • Create invite links for new family members or friends. Each link is single-use, expires in 7 days, and lets whoever clicks it create a fresh account.
  • See pending password resets. When a user taps Forgot password, it shows up here. You copy the reset link and send it to them.
  • Revoke invites or resets you've issued by mistake.

Don't share invite or reset links publicly — they're effectively one-time tickets into an account.

Things that are still rough

Ceòl is a personal project under active development. A few known limitations worth knowing about:

  • AIFF audio files don't import. Convert to MP3 first.
  • iOS silent switch silences playback. Flip the switch on the side of the phone to "loud" if you can't hear anything.
  • The bar-shading on the loop range sometimes doesn't visually highlight properly — playback still loops the right range though.
  • No undo on deletions. Be careful with the bin icon.
  • Sharing tunes between users isn't built yet — if you want to send a tune to someone, ask Callum to do it manually for now.
  • The tuner using your phone's microphone is on the roadmap, not built yet.

If you spot something else, tell Callum. Bug reports are welcome and usually get fixed within a session or two.
